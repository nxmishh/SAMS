Sentry Alert and Monitoring System (SAMS)


SAMS, a cutting-edge system designed to enhance safety and vigilance in various applications. SAMS leverages the power of computer vision and edge computing to monitor the alertness of individuals in real-time. Utilizing Mediapipe's advanced facial mesh technology, SAMS can detect critical indicators of drowsiness or distraction such as yawning, eye closure, and head position. By integrating a Raspberry Pi for processing and a LoRa module for reliable wireless communication, SAMS provides a robust and cost-effective solution for monitoring and alerting in challenging environments.


Alertness Monitoring: Yawning, Eyes Closed and Head Position

Yawning :

SAMS can accurately identify and track yawning events by analyzing the movements of the mouth corners and the jawline. When a prolonged yawn is detected, the system triggers an alert, indicating a potential decrease in alertness.

Eyes Closed :

SAMS monitors eye closure by tracking the position of the eyelids. When the eyes are closed for an extended period, the system sends out an alert to signal potential drowsiness or distraction.

Head Position :

SAMS analyzes head position to identify any significant tilting or nodding, which can be indicative of fatigue or lack of focus. If the system detects consistent head movements outside of a predefined threshold, an alert is triggered.


Applications: Convoy and Sentry Alert and Monitoring

Driver Alertness

SAMS can significantly enhance driver safety within convoys by continuously monitoring the alertness of each driver. Real-time alerts can be sent to convoy leaders or dispatchers if a driver exhibits signs of drowsiness or distraction.

Early Intervention

Early detection of driver fatigue can prevent accidents and minimize disruptions to the convoy. By monitoring alertness levels, SAMS enables timely intervention to ensure the safe operation of the entire convoy.

Enhanced Safety

SAMS contributes to a safer and more efficient convoy operation by minimizing the risk of accidents caused by driver fatigue. It provides a valuable tool for managing driver alertness and improving overall convoy safety.
